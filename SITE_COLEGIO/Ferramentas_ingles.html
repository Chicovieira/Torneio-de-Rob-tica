STEAM Methodology

The term STEAM emerged in the United States in the 90s and is nothing more than the abbreviation for Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, Mathematics, that is, it is a methodology that aims to foster integration in the areas of Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Mathematics.

Through the STEAM Methodology, students can develop important skills for our time, such as critical thinking, creativity, problem solving, collaboration and communication

.

Maker Culture

It is a contemporary subculture that represents an extension of DIY culture (Do It Yourself), but based on technology.

Maker culture can be described as a philosophy in which individuals or groups of individuals create artifacts that are recreated and assembled with the help of software and/or physical objects .
